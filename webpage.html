The Problems With American Education


  • Insufficient Career Exploration Before College
  • Inadequate College Preparation During High School
  • Rising Costs of Higher Education
  • Possible Solutions
  • Organizations that Help Solve this Issue

Education in America has been fairly successful throughout its history in educating youths across the country, preparing them for a lucrative career. However, American education does not do much to help students prepare for higher education and a career, and there are many aspects of the system that can be significantly improved. First, the curriculum does not do much to help students explore career options, often postponing it to after high school and sometimes, even later. Second, the cost for higher education has increased dramatically, resulting in less people receiving college diplomas, threatening economic competition for the country. Third, many students are not adequately prepared for college, with many of them struggling to handle the demands of being in college. Failing to address these issues now will prevent many of those currently in school from reaching their full potential, creating problems for the American work force in the future.
